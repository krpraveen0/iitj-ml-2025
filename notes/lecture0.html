üîπ 1. Linear Algebra

1.1 Vectors

  • A vector = ordered list of numbers.
    $$ \mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \ x_2 \ x_3 \end{bmatrix} $$
    Example: A student‚Äôs marks in 3 subjects: [80, 75, 90].

üëâ Think of vectors as arrows in space pointing from the origin.

1.2 Vector Operations

  • Addition: $$ [1,2] + [3,4] = [4,6] $$

  • Scalar multiplication: $$ 2 \cdot [3,5] = [6,10] $$

  • Dot Product (similarity): $$ \mathbf{x}\cdot \mathbf{y} = \sum_{i=1}^n x_i y_i $$
    Example:
    [1,2] ‚ãÖ [3,4] = (1)(3) + (2)(4) = 11.

  • Norm (Length of vector): $$ |\mathbf{x}| = \sqrt{x_1^2 + x_2^2 + \dots + x_n^2} $$
    Example: ||[3,4]|| = ‚àö(3¬≤+4¬≤) = 5.

1.3 Matrices

A matrix is a table of numbers.
$$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \ 3 & 4 \end{bmatrix} $$

  • Matrix multiplication:
    Multiply row of first √ó column of second.
    $$ \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \ 3 & 4 \end{bmatrix}\cdot\begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 \ 7 & 8 \end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix} 19 & 22 \ 43 & 50 \end{bmatrix} $$

‚úÖ Solved Example

Compute dot product and norm:
$$ x = [2,3], \quad y = [4,5] $$

  • Dot product: 2¬∑4 + 3¬∑5 = 23.
  • Norm of x: ‚àö(2¬≤+3¬≤) = ‚àö13 ‚âà 3.61.

üîπ 2. Calculus

2.1 Derivatives

The derivative measures rate of change (slope of curve).
$$ f'(x) = \frac{df}{dx} $$

  • Example:
    If f(x) = x¬≤, then f'(x) = 2x.
    At x=3, slope = 6.

2.2 Gradients

For functions with many variables:
$$ f(x,y) = x^2 + y^2 $$
Gradient = vector of partial derivatives:
$$ \nabla f(x,y) = \left[ \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}, \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} \right] = [2x, 2y] $$

2.3 Optimization (Gradient Descent)

ML uses calculus to minimize error.
Update rule: $$ \theta := \theta - \alpha \cdot \nabla J(\theta) $$
(where Œ± = learning rate)

‚úÖ Solved Example

Find derivative of:
$$ f(x) = 3x^2 + 2x $$
Solution:
f'(x) = 6x + 2.
At x=2: slope = 6(2)+2 = 14.

üîπ 3. Probability & Statistics

3.1 Basics

  • P(A): probability of event A.
  • P(A ‚à© B): probability of A and B.
  • P(A|B): probability of A given B.

Bayes‚Äô Rule:
$$ P(A|B) = \frac{P(B|A) \cdot P(A)}{P(B)} $$

3.2 Expectation & Variance

  • Expectation: $$ E[X] = \sum x_i P(x_i) $$
    (average value)

  • Variance: $$ Var(X) = E[(X - E[X])^2] $$
    (spread of data)

3.3 Common Distributions

  • Bernoulli: coin flip (0/1).
  • Gaussian (Normal): bell curve.

‚úÖ Solved Example

Roll a fair dice. What is probability of 6?
$$ P(6) = \frac{1}{6} ‚âà 0.1667 $$

If you roll dice 600 times, expected number of 6s = 600 √ó 1/6 = 100.

  3. Apply Bayes‚Äô rule:

import numpy as np

g = x**2 + x*y + y**2
print("Gradient:", [sp.diff(g, var) for var in (x,y)])

# Probability

print("Mean:", np.mean(data))
print("Variance:", np.var(data))
